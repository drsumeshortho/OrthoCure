Beyond Calcium and Vitamin D: Bone Health After 50 in Chennai

By Dr. Sumesh Subramanian , Orthopaedic Surgeon ¬∑ OrthoCure Bone and Joint Speciality Clinic , Thirumullaivoyal ¬∑ 11 Nov 2025 ¬∑ 7 min read

Quick read: Strong bones after 50 need more than milk and a morning walk. This Chennai focused guide covers smart sun exposure, strength and balance work, practical home safety, nutrition that fits our kitchens, and when to talk to your doctor about tests or treatment.

Why this matters for Chennai families

Recent studies show that 80 percent of tested Chennai residents are vitamin D deficient despite year round sunshine, and 42 percent of postmenopausal women have osteoporosis. Many adults spend daylight hours indoors, avoid midday heat, and live in homes with polished floors that become slippery in our humidity. Add reduced daily movement and fast staircases, and you have a quiet recipe for weak bones and preventable falls.

The good news? Small daily changes add up. Whether you are in Thirumullaivoyal, Ambattur, Avadi, or anywhere in North Chennai, this evidence based guide gives you practical steps you can start this week.

Quick self check: Do any of these apply to you?
  • Lost more than 1 inch (2.5 cm) in height
  • Fractured a bone from a simple fall
  • Family history of osteoporosis or hip fractures
  • Avoid midday sun exposure most days
  • Early menopause or long term steroid use

If yes to two or more, consider a bone health consultation to assess your fracture risk.

Step one: simple checks and smart screening

If you are over 50 and have risk factors ‚Äî previous low energy fracture, long term steroid use, early menopause, very low body weight, or strong family history ‚Äî speak to your doctor about a bone density scan (DEXA) and fracture risk calculation using a tool like FRAX.

These tests help decide if lifestyle steps alone are enough or if you may benefit from medical treatment. The FRAX tool is validated for Indian populations and can often stratify your risk without requiring an immediate DEXA scan.

Note: OrthoCure does not perform bone density scans on site. We guide you on when a scan is sensible and help you interpret the report with a clear action plan.

üí° Screening recommendations: Women 65 plus and men 70 plus should get screened. If you are 50 to 69 with risk factors, earlier screening is advised. In India, women over 40 with multiple risk factors should also consider assessment.

Step two: sun the right way in our climate

For many adults here, vitamin D levels are lower than expected despite plenty of sunshine. The time of day, skin coverage, and skin type all matter more than total sun exposure.

The Chennai vitamin D paradox explained

Why are 8 in 10 Chennai residents deficient? Our modern lifestyle keeps us indoors during peak synthesis hours between 11 am and 2 pm. We commute in covered vehicles, work in AC offices, and naturally avoid the intense midday heat. While we are protecting ourselves from discomfort, our bones pay the price.

Practical sun exposure guidance

  • Timing: Aim for 10 to 30 minutes between 11 am and 2 pm, when your shadow is shorter than your height
  • Skin exposure: Forearms and lower legs if possible; keep face protected with a hat
  • Skin tone matters: Darker skin requires longer exposure for the same vitamin D synthesis, up to 3 to 6 times longer than fair skin
  • Stop before pink: Never allow skin to turn pink or burn
  • Frequency: Two to three times weekly is sufficient for maintenance

If testing shows very low vitamin D (many Chennai residents test below 20 ng/mL), your doctor may advise supplements of 800 to 1000 IU daily for a period, followed by a maintenance plan combining sun exposure and lower dose supplementation.

Step three: strength and balance beat steps alone

Walking is excellent for heart health and mood. For bones and fall prevention, you need to add two critical pillars: strength work and balance training. Research shows resistance training increases bone mineral density at the spine and hip by 0.6 to 0.8 percent after just 12 weeks of consistent practice.

Strength work: two to three days per week

These exercises create mechanical stress on bones, stimulating them to become stronger:

  • Chair sit to stand: Builds leg strength and hip stability (start with 8 to 10 reps)
  • Wall push ups: Strengthens arms and wrists without floor pressure
  • Step ups: Use a low, stable step; improves leg power and balance
  • Resistance band rows: Strengthens back and improves posture
  • Resistance band shoulder presses: Builds upper body strength
  • Calf raises: Strengthens lower legs and ankles (important for balance)

Balance practice: daily, in small pockets

Since 90 percent of hip fractures result from falls, balance training is non negotiable. The beauty is you can practice anywhere:

  • Single leg stands: Hold near a counter or wall support while making tea (aim for 20 to 30 seconds per side)
  • Heel to toe walk: Walk in a straight line across a corridor as if on a tightrope
  • Slow sit to stand: From a chair, stand up and sit down slowly without using your hands
  • Tandem stance: Stand with one foot directly in front of the other
üí° Pro tip: Start with just two exercises from each category. Add more as you build confidence over 4 to 6 weeks. Consistency beats intensity for long term bone health.

Important: If you already have very low bone density, spine fractures, or persistent back pain, ask your doctor or a physiotherapist to tailor your exercise plan before starting. Some movements may need modification for safety.

Step four: eat for bones and muscles with local food

Think of bone as living tissue that needs building blocks every day, not just calcium, but a team of nutrients working together.

Calcium rich local foods (target: 1000 mg daily)

  • Ragi (finger millet): Porridge, dosa, or roti ‚Äî one of the best calcium sources
  • Sesame seeds: Add to chutney or sprinkle on dishes
  • Drumstick leaves (murungai keerai): Traditional South Indian option rich in calcium
  • Small fish with bones: Nethili, mathi ‚Äî eat the soft bones for maximum benefit
  • Curd and paneer: Familiar dairy options

Protein for bone matrix (target: 1.0 to 1.2 g per kg body weight)

For a 60 kg person, that is 60 to 72 grams daily. Protein supports both bone structure and muscle strength:

  • Dal and legumes
  • Millets like foxtail and kodo
  • Eggs, fish, chicken in moderation
  • Paneer and thick curd

Supporting nutrients

  • Vitamin D: Fortified milk, egg yolks, fatty fish, plus a supplement after testing if required
  • Magnesium: Leafy greens, nuts, seeds, whole grains
  • Vitamin K: Greens like spinach, amaranth, coriander
‚ö†Ô∏è Watch your salt: Heavy salt can increase calcium loss in urine. Use spices and herbs for flavour and aim for less than one teaspoon daily.

Also moderate alcohol and avoid smoking since both reduce bone density and increase fracture risk.

Step five: fall proof your Chennai home in one weekend

Since 90 percent of hip fractures result from falls ‚Äî and our humidity makes polished floors particularly hazardous ‚Äî your home environment deserves immediate attention.

Bathroom (highest risk area)

  • Anti slip mats inside and outside the shower or bath area
  • Grab bars near the toilet and inside the shower, professionally installed
  • Raised toilet seat if you have knee or hip problems
  • Adequate lighting with a night light
  • Keep floor dry and mop spills immediately

Throughout the home

  • Lighting: Motion sensor night lights in corridors and bathrooms
  • Flooring: Secure or remove loose rugs; dry polished floors quickly after cleaning
  • Stairs: Handrails on both sides; good lighting; consider contrasting tape on edges
  • Clutter: Keep walkways clear of wires and obstacles
  • Kitchen: Store frequently used items at waist height
  • Footwear: Closed back non slip footwear indoors

üí° Weekend project: Walk through your home with a family member and list hazards together. Many fixes cost less than ‚Çπ2000 and can prevent a major hospital bill from a hip fracture.

When lifestyle measures are not enough

Some adults remain at high fracture risk despite good habits, typically with very low bone density, previous fractures, or medical conditions. Your doctor may discuss medicines that slow bone breakdown or build new bone for a period.

Common medication options in India

  • Bisphosphonates (alendronate, risedronate): slow bone breakdown; first line treatment; often used for 3 to 5 years with monitoring
  • Denosumab: Twice yearly injection; useful for patients with kidney concerns
  • Teriparatide: Daily injection that builds bone; reserved for severe cases; maximum two year duration

Treatment is individualized. Your doctor will use tools like FRAX to calculate your 10 year fracture probability and weigh that against medication costs, side effects, and your preferences before recommending any plan.

When to see your orthopaedic doctor

Do not wait for a fracture. Schedule a consultation if you experience:

  • Height loss: More than 1 inch from your maximum adult height
  • Postural changes: A stooped posture or rounded upper back
  • Unexplained back pain: Especially mid to lower back pain that worsens with standing or walking
  • Fracture from minor trauma: Breaking a bone from standing height or less
  • High risk factors: Long term steroid use, early menopause, very low body weight, or strong family history

Key takeaways

  • Screening with DEXA and a FRAX assessment guides wise decisions after 50, especially with risk factors
  • Despite Chennai sunshine, many adults are vitamin D deficient ‚Äî short correct midday sun or a small supplement after testing is usually needed
  • Strength and balance practice protect bones and prevent falls more effectively than steps alone
  • Local foods like ragi, drumstick leaves, sesame, small fish, and millets can meet most nutrition needs
  • Simple home modifications, especially in bathrooms, prevent the majority of serious falls
  • Medicines are reserved for high risk patients and should follow an individualized fracture risk discussion

Frequently asked questions

I drink milk every day. Is that enough for bone strength

Milk provides calcium, but bones also need vitamin D to absorb that calcium, protein for bone matrix, magnesium, and vitamin K. Add strength work and balance practice so your body uses those nutrients to build stronger bone.

Can I reverse osteoporosis with food and exercise alone

Many with osteopenia can improve with daily habits. If you already have osteoporosis or previous fractures, a period of medicine is often needed to reduce fracture risk. Your doctor will guide this after a risk check.

What if I cannot go out in the midday sun

Look for short windows when your shadow is shorter than your height, expose forearms and legs if possible, and consider a small daily supplement after testing. A doctor can personalize the dose and duration.

Do I need a bone density scan

Women 65 plus and men 70 plus usually benefit. If you are 50 to 69 with risk factors, earlier screening is sensible. The scan is done at a diagnostic center. We help you decide and interpret results with a clear plan.
