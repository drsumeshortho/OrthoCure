Why PEACE & LOVE Is Replacing RICE in Injury Recovery

Expert advice from Dr. Sumesh Subramanian | OrthoCure Blog

For decades, RICE (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation) has been the go-to method for managing soft tissue injuries. However, updated guidelines published by Dubois and Esculier in the British Journal of Sports Medicine (2020) suggest a more holistic, evidence-based approach: PEACE & LOVE.

🚫 Why RICE Is Now Considered Outdated

  • Ice: May reduce pain, but can impair local circulation and delay healing.
  • Rest: Prolonged immobility leads to joint stiffness and muscle atrophy.
  • Anti-inflammatories: Suppress the body’s natural healing cascade.

Modern recovery focuses on active participation and cellular healing — not just symptom suppression.

✅ PEACE & LOVE: The New Gold Standard

Source: Dubois B, Esculier JF. Br J Sports Med. 2020

🕊 PEACE (Immediate Care)

  • P – Protect: Minimize movement and avoid pain triggers in the first 1–3 days.
  • E – Elevate: Elevate injured limb above heart level to reduce edema.
  • A – Avoid Anti-inflammatories: NSAIDs may interfere with tissue repair.
  • C – Compress: Use elastic bandages or sleeves to support and reduce swelling.
  • E – Educate: Patients should understand their injury, recovery process, and realistic timelines.

❤️ LOVE (Subacute to Chronic Phase)

  • L – Load: Initiate gradual loading and movement early to promote tissue remodeling.
  • O – Optimism: Patient mindset plays a measurable role in pain and outcome.
  • V – Vascularisation: Introduce pain-free aerobic activities like walking, cycling.
  • E – Exercise: Targeted rehab plans restore strength, balance, and function.

🧠 Practical Tips If You’re Injured

  • Avoid overusing ice and painkillers.
  • Don’t rest too long — controlled activity is better.
  • Seek orthopaedic advice early to guide healing.
  • Start physiotherapy when safe, ideally within a week.

📌 Common Questions

Does PEACE & LOVE apply to fractures?

No — it’s designed for soft tissue injuries. Fractures need immobilization, realignment, or surgery.

How soon can I begin rehab?

As early as 48–72 hours post-injury, under supervision. Early loading improves long-term results.

Can children follow this protocol?

Yes, with modified supervision. It encourages safe movement without medication overuse.
