The Myth: “Walking Damages Arthritic Knees”

Many believe walking wears down arthritic knees. In truth, movement nourishes cartilage and keeps surrounding muscles active. Inactivity worsens stiffness and pain.

Why Walking Helps

Lubrication

Walking circulates synovial fluid — easing stiffness.

Muscle Strength

Strengthens thigh and calf muscles that support the knee.

Weight & Metabolism

Helps control weight and inflammation markers.

Pain Modulation

Activates natural pain relief pathways over time.

How Much & How Often

Aim for 150 minutes/week or 6–8k steps/day. Build gradually and use pain as your guide (≤4/10, resolved within 24h).

Safe Walking Tips

  • Use even, shaded routes — parks or tracks.
  • Wear cushioned shoes; replace worn soles.
  • Warm up 3–5 mins; stretch post-walk.
  • Progress slowly; +5 mins every few sessions.
  • In Chennai heat, walk mornings/evenings and hydrate well.

When to Slow Down or Modify

  • Pain >5/10 or lasting >24–36h
  • Swelling, warmth, or joint instability
  • Night pain or rapid worsening

FAQs

Is walking harmful if I have knee osteoarthritis?

No. Moderate walking supports joint health and reduces stiffness.

Which surface and shoes are best?

Even, shock-absorbing surfaces and cushioned shoes are ideal.

How much should I walk?

Work toward 150 mins/week or 6–8k steps/day.

What if my knees hurt more after walking?

Reduce speed/distance, and review with your doctor if pain persists.
