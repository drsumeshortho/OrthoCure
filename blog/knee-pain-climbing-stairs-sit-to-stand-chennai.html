Knee Pain While Climbing Stairs and Getting Up From a Chair

If your knee pain is worse on stairs or when standing up from a chair, it is often patellofemoral pain. With the right plan, most cases improve without surgery.

Front knee pain on stairs or while getting up from a chair is one of the most common knee complaints. It often shows up in office workers, homemakers, and anyone whose day includes long sitting followed by sudden activity.

Most people worry about two things: Is this arthritis? Will I need surgery?
In many patients, the answer is no. What you usually need is clarity, a sensible load plan, and targeted strengthening.

At OrthoCure Bone and Joint Speciality Clinic, Thirumullaivoyal, I see this pattern daily in patients from Ambattur, Avadi, Annanur, Iyyapakkam, and nearby areas. Many patients try rest and pain tablets, feel better briefly, and then the pain returns the moment stairs or floor level sitting comes back. The long term fix is usually not a stronger tablet. It is a better plan: better movement control, stronger hip and thigh muscles, and smarter progression of load.

If you have swelling, locking, repeated giving way, or pain after a fall, get examined early.
