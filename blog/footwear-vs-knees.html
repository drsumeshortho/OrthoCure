Footwear vs Knees: Simple Shoe Fixes That Reduce Pain

By Dr. Sumesh Subramanian • OrthoCure Bone & Joint Speciality Clinic, Thirumullaivoyal

Flat, flimsy slippers overload your knees during daily walking and standing—especially on hard Chennai floors. The fix isn’t fancy insoles for everyone; it’s **stable, cushioned footwear** and a few habit tweaks. Here’s the short, clinic-tested playbook you can use today.

3 quick fixes that help most knees

  1. Switch from ultra-flat to stable cushioning. Look for a **firm heel counter** + **midfoot support**. House sandals with a slight rocker help on hard floors.
  2. Retire worn-out soles. If you walk daily, replace shoes every **9–12 months** or when tread is visibly uneven.
  3. Shorten painful walks, not movement. Swap long, painful walks for **short, frequent bouts** + **basic strengthening** (sit-to-stand, mini-squats within comfort).
PS: Bring your current footwear to clinic. We’ll do a 2-minute bend/heel test and show you what to change.

Buying guide: what to look for

  • Heel counter: should resist collapse when pinched.
  • Midfoot: mild torsional rigidity (not floppy).
  • Forefoot: bends at the toe, not mid-arch.
  • Stack height: modest cushioning; avoid paper-thin soles for long standing.
  • Fit: thumb’s width of space in front of longest toe.

Custom orthotics are **not** for everyone. We consider them only after a clinical exam and gait assessment.

Home & office tweaks (low effort, real payoff)

  • Use **anti-fatigue mats** in kitchen/standing zones.
  • At office, park a **supportive pair** under your desk; swap out of dress shoes when seated.
  • Do **30–60 seconds** of calf raises and sit-to-stands every 2–3 hours.

Who should get checked first?

  • Persistent medial knee pain despite better footwear
  • History of meniscus/ligament issues
  • Frequent swelling or locking/catching

FAQ

Are flat slippers bad for knee arthritis?

Long, daily standing/walking in ultra-flat slippers can increase knee load. Switch to stable, cushioned footwear first; add strengthening.
