Quick Fact

Keep the backpack at or under 10% of your child’s body weight. This simple rule reduces strain on growing spines and helps prevent pain.

What a good fit looks like

  • Use both straps (wide & padded).
  • Adjust so the bag sits high & close to the back; not below the waist.
  • Optional chest/waist strap for better weight distribution.

Ideal school bag features

  • Lightweight bag with padded back + shoulder straps.
  • Multiple compartments to balance weight.
  • Side pocket for water bottle; reflective strips for road safety.
  • Backpack height: not taller than your child’s torso.

Pack Smart

  • Place heaviest books closest to the back panel.
  • Use compartments to balance the load.
  • Carry only essentials; clean out weekly.

Lift Safely

  • Bend at the knees to pick up the backpack.
  • Wear both straps before standing fully upright.
  • If cycling, keep the bag snug and avoid extra loads on the bike.

Watch for Red Flags

  • Persistent back, neck, or shoulder pain.
  • Tingling or numbness in arms.
  • Visible strap marks or leaning forward to compensate.
