Leg Pain That Travels Down the Leg

A calm, conservative guide to sciatica and nerve related leg pain for daily commuters and workers in North Chennai.
By Dr Sumesh Subramanian, Orthopaedic Surgeon

Home › Blog › Leg pain and sciatica
Sharp or electric pain travelling from the back or hip into the leg often causes fear. Most cases recover without surgery when managed stepwise and early.

What Sciatica Means

Sciatica is a symptom, not a diagnosis. It refers to irritation of the sciatic nerve anywhere along its path.

Clinical point: Not all leg pain comes from the spine.

Common Causes

  • Lumbar disc irritation
  • Piriformis muscle tightness
  • Prolonged sitting or riding
  • Weak core and poor posture

The MRI Confusion

Disc bulges are common even in people without pain. Imaging must match symptoms.

Clinic principle: Treat the patient, not the report.

Stepwise Conservative Plan

Step 1: Calm inflammation

Posture advice and short term medication.

Step 2: Physiotherapy

Nerve mobility and core strengthening.

Step 3: Injections (selected cases)

Only when pain blocks rehab.

Step 4: Surgery

Last option when red flags or persistent symptoms exist.

When to Seek Urgent Care

Sudden weakness, numbness in the groin, or bladder and bowel changes need immediate evaluation.

Leg Pain Affecting Daily Life?

Most patients improve with conservative care.
