The common fear: Sharp or electric pain travelling from the back or hip into the leg.

The common worry: “Is this a slipped disc? Do I need spine surgery?”

The reality: In most people, leg pain improves with conservative, stepwise care.

What Sciatica Actually Means

Sciatica is not a diagnosis. It is a symptom.

When the sciatic nerve is irritated anywhere along its path, pain can travel from the lower back or hip into the thigh, leg or foot. However, not all leg pain is caused by a slipped disc.

Clinical point: Many patients have leg pain without dangerous nerve compression.

Common Causes of Leg Pain in Our Area

  • Lumbar disc bulge with nerve irritation
  • Piriformis muscle tightness (false sciatica)
  • Prolonged sitting or riding over uneven roads
  • Poor posture and weak core muscles

The MRI Confusion

Many people panic after reading MRI reports that mention disc bulges. Large studies show that disc changes are common even in people without pain.

Clinic principle: We treat the patient and symptoms, not the scan alone.

Stepwise Conservative Plan

Step 1: Calm the Irritation

Activity modification, posture advice and short term medication if needed.

Step 2: Physiotherapy

Nerve mobility exercises, hip flexibility and core strengthening.

Step 3: Precision Injections (Selected Cases)

Used only when pain blocks rehabilitation.

Step 4: Surgery (Last Option)

Considered only when symptoms persist or red flags appear.

When to Seek Immediate Care

  • Sudden weakness or foot drop
  • Numbness in the groin or inner thighs
  • Loss of bladder or bowel control

Leg Pain Affecting Walking or Work?

Most cases improve without surgery when managed early and correctly.
