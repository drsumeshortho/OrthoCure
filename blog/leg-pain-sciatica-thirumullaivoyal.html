What Sciatica Means

Sciatica is a symptom, not a diagnosis. It describes pain that travels along the sciatic nerve, which runs from the lower back into the leg.

Some people have true nerve root irritation from the lower back. Others have pain that mimics sciatica from hip and buttock muscles. The treatment depends on which pattern you have.

Clinic approach: We first identify the pattern by examination and movement testing. Then we choose a stepwise plan.

Common Causes in Our Area

  • Lumbar disc irritation with nerve inflammation
  • Piriformis muscle tightness causing false sciatica
  • Prolonged sitting with poor hip mobility
  • Weak core and poor movement control
Practical point: Many cases improve once we reduce irritation and rebuild movement confidence.

The MRI Confusion

Many people panic when they see disc bulge or disc protrusion on a report. The scan needs to match symptoms and examination. Disc changes are also seen in people who have no pain.

When imaging is useful

  • Persistent symptoms despite structured conservative care
  • Progressive weakness or worrying neurological signs
  • When we need to rule out uncommon causes

Stepwise Conservative Plan

The goal is not bed rest. The goal is safe movement, reduced irritation, and rebuilding strength over time.

Step 1: Calm irritation

Activity modification, posture guidance, and short term medication if needed.

Step 2: Physiotherapy

Nerve mobility work, hip flexibility, and core stability. See physiotherapy in Thirumullaivoyal.

Step 3: Precision injections

Selected cases only, usually to help continue rehabilitation when pain blocks progress.

Step 4: Surgery

Discussed only when symptoms persist or red flags are present.

Clinical evidence behind this approach
  • Most low back pain and sciatica improve with conservative care and time (Lancet Low Back Pain Series)
  • Disc changes on imaging can be present even without pain (AJNR review on asymptomatic degeneration)
  • Guidelines recommend conservative care first, with escalation only when needed (NICE and NASS guidance)

When Urgent Care Is Needed

Red flags

  • Sudden weakness, foot drop, or progressive weakness
  • Numbness in the groin region
  • Bladder or bowel control changes

If these occur, do not self treat. Get evaluated urgently.

Patient Questions

Does leg pain always mean a slipped disc

No. Muscle tightness, hip irritation, posture problems, and prolonged sitting can cause leg pain without a dangerous disc problem.

Is sciatica a diagnosis

Sciatica is a symptom. The underlying cause needs clinical assessment.

Do I need an MRI

Not always. MRI is considered when symptoms and examination suggest nerve compression or when symptoms persist despite proper conservative care.

Can sciatica recover without surgery

Yes. Most cases improve with physiotherapy, graded activity, posture correction, and time.

What is piriformis syndrome

It is irritation of the sciatic nerve by a tight buttock muscle. It often mimics sciatica and usually responds well to physiotherapy.
