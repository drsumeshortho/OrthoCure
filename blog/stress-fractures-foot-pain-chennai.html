At OrthoCure Bone and Joint Speciality Clinic in Thirumullaivoyal we often see people who have unknowingly walked on a stress fracture for many days. Early X rays can appear normal which gives a false sense of reassurance. By the time they arrive the crack has already progressed.

What Is a Stress Fracture

A stress fracture is a small crack in the bone caused by repeated stress rather than a single injury. Bone remodels daily. When demand exceeds repair capacity microcracks form. Continued load deepens the crack and produces localised pain.

  • Metatarsals
  • Tibia
  • Heel bone
  • Hip region in active adults and older adults
  • Navicular and other midfoot bones

For residents of Ambattur Avadi Annanur and Thirumullaivoyal daily walking on hard floors thin footwear and low vitamin D create the perfect setup.

How Stress Fractures Start

Sudden jump in activity

Increases load faster than bone can adapt.

Weak bones or low vitamin D

Common in Chennai despite abundant sunlight.

Daily repetitive impact

Standing jobs and hard floors increase stress.

Poor cushioning

Thin chappals worn out soles and concrete transfer load directly to bone.

Ignoring early signals

Mild pain becomes a deeper crack when neglected.

Typical Symptoms

  • Pain that increases with walking and settles with rest
  • Localised tenderness at one point
  • Mild swelling or warmth
  • Pain on hopping or single leg stance
  • Earlier morning onset as days progress

Who Is At Higher Risk

  • Women with vitamin D deficiency
  • Adults above forty with low bone density
  • People with sudden increase in walking or gym activity
  • Standing job professionals
  • People with previous fractures or osteoporosis

How We Confirm The Diagnosis

Early X rays may appear normal. When we suspect a stress fracture we use:

  • Clinical examination
  • X rays
  • MRI when clarity is needed
  • Vitamin D and calcium tests
  • Bone density scan when indicated

The goal is early confirmation and protection before the crack deepens.

Treatment How Stress Fractures Heal

Most stress fractures heal well when protected early.

  1. Reduce load rest footwear support brace if needed
  2. Improve bone health vitamin D correction and osteoporosis screening
  3. Gradual return to activity guided by our physiotherapy team

Prevention Tips

  • Build leg and hip strength
  • Practice balance routinely
  • Replace worn-out footwear
  • Increase walking distance gradually
  • Check vitamin D and bone density above age fifty
  • Monitor any localised pain while walking

When To See An Orthopaedic Doctor

  • Pain lasting more than five to seven days with walking
  • Sharp pain at a specific point
  • Pain on hopping
  • Swelling without injury
  • Adults over forty with low bone density

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a stress fracture turn into a complete fracture

Yes continued load can progress the crack.

Do I always need an MRI

No MRI is used selectively for early confirmation.

How long is rest needed

Six to eight weeks with gradual progression.

Can stress fractures be prevented

Yes with strength balance footwear support and gradual activity build up.
