Fall Prevention After 60

Orthopaedic guidance for safer ageing | Dr Sumesh Subramanian

A fall after the age of 60 is not just an accident. It is often the first sign that balance, strength, vision, reaction time, or bone health needs attention.

One fall can change daily independence but most falls are preventable with simple steps.

Studies show that nearly one in three adults above 60 experience at least one fall every year. Many falls happen at home and result in fractures, prolonged pain, or fear of walking.

Why homes become high risk after 60

  • Slippery flooring in bathrooms and kitchens
  • Poor lighting at night
  • Loose rugs, wires, or cluttered walking paths
  • Lack of hand support near toilets or stairs

Simple home changes that reduce fall risk

Bathroom safety

  • Use anti skid mats or textured flooring
  • Install grab bars near toilet and shower
  • Keep floors dry

Bedroom and living area

  • Remove loose rugs
  • Use night lamps
  • Keep daily use items within easy reach

Staircase safety

  • Good lighting
  • Handrails on both sides
  • Clear edges

Balance and strength matter more than age

Muscle weakness and poor balance are major contributors to falls. Simple strength and balance exercises can reduce fall risk significantly.

Physiotherapy guided balance training also helps people with knee arthritis and post fracture recovery. Learn about physiotherapy at OrthoCure.

Medical causes of falls you should not ignore

  • Low blood sugar in diabetes
  • Low blood pressure on standing
  • Heart rhythm problems
  • Stroke or mini stroke
  • Inner ear balance disorders

Recurrent falls with shoulder pain or stiffness should also be checked for frozen shoulder. Frozen shoulder care.

Repeated unexplained falls always need medical evaluation.

Why falls after 60 often cause fractures

  • Hip fractures leading to prolonged bed rest
  • Wrist fractures while breaking a fall
  • Spine fractures even with minor trauma
  • Shoulder fractures after sideways falls

Early assessment helps prevent missed fractures.

What to do immediately after a fall

  • Do not rush to stand up
  • Check hip, wrist, shoulder and back pain
  • Seek help if pain or deformity is present
  • Get assessed if pain increases over 24 hours
Persistent pain after a fall should always be checked.

When should you see an orthopaedic doctor

  • Two or more falls in six months
  • Persistent joint pain after a fall
  • Known osteoporosis
