Monsoon & Joint Pain in Chennai: What’s Real, What Helps

By Dr. Sumesh | MS Ortho, Fellowship in Arthroplasty

TL;DR Weather doesn’t cause arthritis—but humidity and pressure changes can nudge pain and stiffness, especially in knees, hips, and hands. Biggest culprit in monsoon? We move less.

Why joints feel worse when it rains

Day‑to‑day changes in barometric pressure and humidity can modestly increase pain in people with existing joint issues. Cooler, damp weather plus reduced sunlight and movement add to the “stiff” feeling. Translation: weather is a nudge—not destiny.

The practical monsoon plan (elder‑friendly, apartment‑friendly)

Do this daily (5–7 minutes, twice a day)

  • Indoor movement loop: march in place → sit‑to‑stand ×10 → heel raises ×10 → mini‑squats to chair ×10 → gentle knee bends ×10 → wall shoulder slides ×10.
  • Warmth therapy: a warm compress to stiff joints for 10–15 minutes (morning/evening).
  • Indoor steps: 1000–2000 steps inside your corridor/home on wet days.
  • Footwear: non‑slip, well‑cushioned footwear at home; avoid barefoot on wet floors.
  • Vitamin D/meds: don’t skip prescribed meds; discuss Vitamin D if aches persist.

Don’t

  • Don’t stop moving for days because it’s raining—deconditioning hurts more than the weather.
  • Don’t rely only on balms/oils; pair warmth with a graded home exercise routine.
  • Don’t ignore slips/falls—get evaluated if pain/swelling follows.

Red flags — get checked this week

  • Persistent swelling, warmth, or redness in a joint.
  • Clicks with pain, locking, or instability.
  • Night pain that wakes you, fever, or unexplained weight loss.
  • New fall or sudden severe pain after a slip.

Quick FAQs (Tamil + English)

Does monsoon weather cause arthritis?

No. Weather doesn’t cause arthritis. It can modestly nudge symptoms in people who already have joint issues.

Why do my knees feel “tight” when it rains?

Humidity and pressure shifts can increase tissue swelling and stiffness. Less movement on wet days adds to it.

What can I do at home?

Short indoor movement twice daily, warmth 10–15 minutes, non‑slip footwear, continue prescribed meds, consider Vitamin D with medical advice.
