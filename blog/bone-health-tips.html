Bone Health Tips Every Adult Should Know

Whether you’ve just done a BMD scan or are looking to keep your bones strong as you age, this guide gives you simple, daily tips you can follow at home. Everything here is adapted for our South Indian lifestyle — from food to exercises.

🩻 Understanding Your BMD Report

  • T-score above -1.0: Normal
  • T-score between -1.0 and -2.5: Osteopenia (low bone density)
  • T-score below -2.5: Osteoporosis (high fracture risk)

If your BMD is low, don’t panic. With the right food, exercise and medical advice, you can improve your bone strength.

🍲 Bone-Strengthening South Indian Foods

Here are calcium and vitamin D-rich foods that are elderly-friendly and easy to add to your meals:

  • Ragi (கேழ்வரகு): 1 cup porridge = 300 mg calcium
  • Drumstick leaves (முருங்கைக் கீரை): Cooked, very high in calcium & iron
  • Sesame seeds (எள்): 1 tbsp podi = ~90 mg calcium
  • Soaked almonds 4–6 per day (பாதாம்): Easy snack, ~50 mg calcium
  • Curd / Cow’s milk (தயிர் / பால்) : 1 cup = ~100–120 mg calcium
  • Sardines, Anchovy Fish (மத்தி மீனு, நெத்திலி மீன்): Excellent calcium & vitamin D source when eaten with bones
  • Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை) : Everyday herb, micronutrient-rich

Include a mix of these daily. Example: Ragi dosa + curd lunch + sesame podi dinner = full-day coverage!

☀️ Vitamin D – The Sunshine Nutrient

  • Expose arms and legs to mid-morning to early afternoon sun (between 10 AM and 1 PM) for 10–30 minutes, 3–5 days a week.
  • Include eggs (yolk), mushrooms, fatty fish, and fortified milk if advised

Supplements may be needed if levels are very low — check with your doctor.

🏃 Easy Exercises to Strengthen Bones

  • Chair sit-stand (10x): Build leg strength
  • Toe raises (15x): Strengthen calf and ankle bones
  • Wall push-ups (10x): Safe upper body loading

Do these 5–6 days a week. Avoid high-impact jumping if bones are weak.

⚠️ Things to Avoid

  • Smoking, alcohol, and fizzy drinks
  • Skipping meals or too much fasting
  • Sitting for long hours without movement
  • Self-medicating with painkillers or steroids

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I reverse bone loss with food alone?
No, but diet plays a huge role in slowing further loss. Your doctor may recommend calcium/vitamin D or other meds.
Should I stop walking if I have joint pain?
No. Modify your walk time or use assistive devices. Gentle movement is better than full rest.
Can I take calcium tablets on my own?
Always check with your doctor — excess calcium has risks too.
