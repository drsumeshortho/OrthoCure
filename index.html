Why patients choose OrthoCure

Neighbourhood orthopaedic care that stays practical

The aim is not to make treatment sound dramatic. It is to explain the problem clearly, choose the right next step, and help patients recover with a plan that fits real life.

Conservative first judgement

Medicines, physiotherapy, braces, activity correction, and stepwise rehabilitation are discussed first whenever reasonable before escalating treatment. See the clinical approach.

Clear explanations

Patients are told what the scan means, what it does not mean, and what the realistic options are. That matters more than noisy medical language.

Local practicality

Advice is shaped around stairs, two wheeler travel, long standing work, floor sitting, and daily movement patterns common around Ambattur, Avadi, and Thirumullaivoyal.

Fracture support with digital X ray

For many fresh injuries and fracture follow ups, digital X ray support improves speed and clarity of assessment at the clinic.

Evening and Sunday consultation

Clinic timings are designed for real working life: Monday to Saturday, 5 PM to 9 PM, and Sunday 11 AM to 1 PM.

Second opinions welcome

If you have already been advised surgery or are confused by MRI or X ray findings, you do not need to guess. A calmer second opinion can help. Read more.

Common questions

Do I need a referral to consult Dr Sumesh?
No. You can call or WhatsApp directly. Walk ins are welcome during clinic hours, though calling ahead helps if you are bringing reports.
Are digital X rays available at the clinic?
Yes. Digital X ray support is available at the clinic for faster and clearer orthopaedic assessment.
Is surgery always the first option?
No. Conservative first care is preferred whenever reasonable. You can also read the clinical approach.
Can I get a second opinion on MRI or X ray reports?
Yes. Bring your reports, prior prescriptions, and any existing advice. You can also visit the Second Opinion page.
