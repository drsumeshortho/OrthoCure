Consultation pathways

Choose the right path for your situation

Not every orthopaedic concern needs the same route. Use the option that fits your current need best.

🏥 Recommended

In clinic consultation

The right choice for most bone and joint concerns, especially when examination, swelling assessment, gait review, or X ray may be needed.

  • Fresh pain, swelling, or limp
  • Fall, twist injury, or suspected fracture
  • Knee, back, shoulder, neck, or heel pain not settling
  • Cases where function matters more than guesswork
📋 Available

Second opinion and report review

Useful if you already have an X ray, MRI, previous treatment advice, or a surgery recommendation and want a clearer orthopaedic review.

  • Conflicting advice from different places
  • Wanted more clarity before surgery
  • Treatment not improving symptoms
  • Need a practical next step, not panic
📞 Limited

Callback or online guidance

Best for preliminary guidance, follow up clarification, or reviewing whether you should come in directly. Subject to doctor availability.

  • Report discussion or follow up doubt
  • Medication or rehab clarification
  • When travel is temporarily difficult
  • Not a replacement for urgent injury examination
Call first if urgent

Fresh injury or severe pain outside clinic hours?

If there is severe pain after a fall, visible deformity, rapid swelling, inability to bear weight, or an open wound, please call immediately.

  • Sudden severe pain following a fall or accident
  • Visible deformity of a limb or joint
  • Inability to bear weight or move the limb properly
  • Rapid swelling and bruising after trauma
  • Open wound near a bone or joint
Basic emergency assessment and X rays may be needed first. Based on the situation, further orthopaedic review and coordination will be guided appropriately.
