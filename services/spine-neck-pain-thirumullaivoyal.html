Spine and Neck Pain Clinic

At OrthoCure Bone and Joint Speciality Clinic spine and neck problems are treated with a conservative first plan. The focus is on finding the real source of pain, explaining it in simple language and building a safe programme of posture change, exercise and physiotherapy. Surgery is discussed only when there is clear evidence that it is needed.

Common spine and neck problems we see

  • Acute low back pain after lifting, travel or sudden movements.
  • Disc prolapse with pain going down the leg or arm.
  • Sciatica with shooting pain from back to buttock and leg.
  • Cervical spondylosis with neck pain and stiffness.
  • Mechanical posture pain from long sitting and poor ergonomics.
  • Neck strain from mobile use, laptop work and long screen time.

Many of these conditions can be managed well with clear advice, exercise and time. The challenge is to pick who needs only rest and who needs further tests.

When spine or neck pain needs a check

  • Pain that lasts more than one to two weeks or keeps returning.
  • Pain that is severe at night or disturbs sleep.
  • Pain going below the knee or into the arm with tingling or numbness.
  • Weakness in foot or hand grip, difficulty walking straight.
  • History of serious trauma, weight loss or fever along with pain.

Sudden loss of bladder or bowel control, rapidly worsening weakness or trauma with inability to move are emergencies and need hospital care without delay.

How we assess spine and neck pain

  • Detailed history of work pattern, posture, travel and previous episodes.
  • Examination of spine movement, nerve tension and muscle strength.
  • Simple tests such as X ray when needed to rule out serious causes.
  • MRI and advanced imaging only when results will change the plan.
  • Review of other medical problems such as diabetes and osteoporosis.

The main aim is to separate simple mechanical pain from disc related nerve problems and rare serious causes.

Stepwise treatment plan

  • Advice on short rest, correct positions for lying, sitting and turning.
  • Gentle early movement to prevent stiffness and fear of activity.
  • Medicines for pain and nerve symptoms in short controlled courses.
  • Physiotherapy with targeted exercises once acute pain is under control.
  • Referral for injections or surgery only when there is clear benefit expected.

Most patients in Ambattur, Avadi and Thirumullaivoyal improve with this stepwise approach without needing an operation.

Posture and daily routine changes

  • Setting safe sitting times with planned standing and stretch breaks.
  • Guidance on chair height, desk level and screen position.
  • Advice on mobile use, neck angle and safe ways to read or watch content.
  • How to lift objects and bend from hips and knees instead of spine alone.
  • Simple routines to follow at home on busy days.

Small consistent changes in posture often give more lasting relief than repeated tablets.

Role of physiotherapy at OrthoCure

Physiotherapy is closely linked with spine and neck care at our clinic. The aim is to strengthen support muscles, improve flexibility and rebuild confidence in movement.

  • Core strengthening for low back pain and disc related issues.
  • Neck and shoulder girdle training for cervical spondylosis and tech neck.
  • Stretching of tight muscle groups such as hamstrings and hip flexors.
  • Balance and gait work where nerve symptoms have caused weakness.

When spine surgery is discussed

  • Severe nerve compression with weakness or loss of control that is not improving.
  • Persistent leg or arm pain due to disc prolapse after a proper trial of conservative care.
  • Spinal canal narrowing with clear matching symptoms and walking limitation.
  • Structural problems such as significant slip or deformity with nerve involvement.

In such situations Dr Sumesh explains options in plain language and, when surgery is likely to help more than harm, guides patients to the right centre and timing. There is no pressure to rush decisions.

Common questions about spine and neck pain

Do I need an MRI scan for every back or neck pain episode

No. Many simple episodes settle with rest, medicine and exercise. MRI is advised when pain is severe, persistent, associated with nerve symptoms or when the result will change the treatment plan.

Is bed rest the best treatment for low back pain

Short periods of relative rest can help during very painful days. Prolonged bed rest often leads to more stiffness and fear of movement. Controlled return to gentle activity is usually better.

Do all disc problems need surgery

No. Many disc prolapse cases improve with time, medicine, physiotherapy and posture correction. Surgery is reserved for cases with ongoing severe pain, progressive weakness or serious nerve signs.

Can neck pain from mobiles and laptops be reversed

In many patients pain from posture and screen use improves once position, duration and exercise are corrected. Lasting change needs both habit change and strengthening, not medicine alone.
