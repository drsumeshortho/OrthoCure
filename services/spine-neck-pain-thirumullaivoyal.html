Treatment approach

  1. Comprehensive assessment that reviews daily activity sleep posture and red flags
  2. Imaging when required with X ray first and MRI for nerve or disc suspicion
  3. Plan with pain control early movement targeted rehab and simple work and sleep changes
  4. Interventional options such as trigger point and nerve block injections in select cases
  5. Follow up with progress tracking and timely escalation or de escalation

Frequently asked questions

What are the most common causes of back or neck pain
Disc bulge spondylosis muscle spasm posture strain and nerve compression are most common. Trauma and other illness are less common but important to rule out.
Do I need bed rest or surgery
No. Most people get better faster with guided activity and good habits. Surgery is for severe weakness loss of control or pain that does not respond to careful care.
Will I feel better quickly
Many patients feel better within a few days. Full recovery often needs one to eight weeks based on the condition and prior episodes.
Are exercises and rehab important
They are vital. Strength mobility and posture training reduce pain and prevent future flares.
