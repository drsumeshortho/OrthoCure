Fracture Care in Thirumullaivoyal

Prompt assessment, X-ray when required, proper immobilisation, and clear guidance — without unnecessary hospital visits.

When should you go directly to a hospital Emergency Room? If the bone is visible through the skin, there is heavy bleeding, loss of consciousness, severe chest pain, or breathing difficulty — please go to a major hospital ER immediately.

Is it a fracture or just a sprain?

  • Did you hear a snap or crack at the time of injury?
  • Is there sudden swelling or bruising?
  • Does the limb look bent or misaligned?
  • Is weight-bearing or movement impossible?

How fractures are treated at our clinic

Not every fracture needs surgery. Many heal well with the right support, protection, and follow-up.

Splints

Temporary support used initially to allow swelling to settle safely.

Plaster of Paris (POP)

Traditional, well-moulded casts for stable fracture healing.

Fiberglass Casts

Lighter, stronger casts with better durability.

Functional Braces

Removable supports that allow early movement when appropriate.

Common questions

Do you have X-ray facilities at the clinic?

Yes. Digital X-ray is available for patients being evaluated, only when clinically required.

Do all fractures need surgery?

No. Many fractures heal completely with plaster or splint support alone.

How long does a cast usually stay on?

Most fractures require 4 to 6 weeks of immobilisation, depending on the bone and fracture type.
