Common fractures treated at OrthoCure

  • Wrist fractures including distal radius and Colles
  • Forearm fractures
  • Clavicle or collarbone fractures
  • Ankle and foot fractures
  • Metacarpal and finger fractures
  • Early stress fractures

What to do before you reach the clinic

  • Elevate the limb and use a cold pack for ten to fifteen minutes at a time.
  • Do not try to pull or set the bone yourself.
  • Remove rings and watches from a swollen hand.
  • Bring any previous X rays or reports if available.

Emergency note: open wounds, loss of sensation or severe deformity need immediate hospital care.

Fracture care questions

Do you have X ray at the clinic?

Yes. The digital X ray unit at OrthoCure allows quick and accurate diagnosis, so treatment decisions are not delayed.

Will every fracture need surgery?

No. Many fractures heal well with proper reduction and firm plaster or cast support. Surgery is advised only when the joint or bone position will not stay stable with conservative treatment alone.

How long does a cast stay on?

Most fractures need three to six weeks of immobilisation depending on the bone and pattern of injury. You will be told when to start gentle movement and physiotherapy.

Are Sunday consultations available?

Yes. The clinic is open on Sunday from 11 am to 1 pm for reviews and planned follow up visits.
