Is it a sprain or a fracture

It is often hard to tell without an X ray. Visit for an assessment if you notice any of the following.

  • A snap crack sensation at the time of injury
  • Immediate swelling or bruising
  • Visible deformity or crooked appearance
  • Unable to bear weight on the leg or foot

On site digital X ray is available when required for diagnosis.

How we stabilise fractures

Not every fracture needs surgery. Many heal well with the right immobilisation, padding, and follow up plan.

Splinting first when swelling is high

Used soon after injury to protect the bone while allowing swelling to settle safely.

Plaster cast

Traditional and reliable. Useful when careful moulding matters for alignment.

Fiberglass cast

Lightweight and durable. Suitable cases can be discussed after examination.

Functional brace or boot

Selected fractures can use removable support with safe guided movement.

Common questions

Do you have X ray facilities at the clinic

Yes. We have digital X ray for patients being evaluated at the clinic when required for diagnosis and decision making.

Do all fractures need surgery

No. Many fractures heal well with plaster cast or splint support. Surgery is discussed only when clearly needed for stability and function.

How long does a cast stay on

Healing time depends on the bone and fracture pattern. Many upper limb fractures need about 4 to 6 weeks, and some ankle fractures need around 6 weeks. Follow up is planned based on your case.

What should I do before coming to the clinic

Support the limb, elevate if possible, use a cold pack briefly, avoid forced movements, and bring any previous X rays or discharge summaries if available.
