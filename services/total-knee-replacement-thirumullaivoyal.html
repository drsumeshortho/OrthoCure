Frequently Asked Questions

When do you need total knee replacement?
When knee pain, stiffness, or swelling limits daily life after medication, physiotherapy, and injections have failed and X-rays show advanced arthritis or deformity.
What is recovery like after TKR?
Most patients walk with support within a day or two and progress to pain-free, independent walking over 4–8 weeks. Success relies on guided physiotherapy and home exercises.
Is TKR safe for those with diabetes or heart issues?
Yes—proper preparation, hospital admission, and medical management can ensure safety, but careful workup and coordinated care are essential. Individual advice given at your consult.
How long will my implant last?
Modern implants can last 15–25 years with good function, depending on activity level, weight, and regular checks after surgery.
How much does TKR cost, and is it covered by insurance?
Packages vary by implant, hospital, insurance, and special needs (single/both knees, comorbidities). All estimates and paperwork are discussed openly at your pre-op plan visit.
