Important: This is a clinic linked service. We advise X rays only after clinical examination, when it changes your treatment plan.

Why on site digital X ray helps

  • Quicker decisions for many fractures
  • Less waiting and less travel
  • Views chosen based on your symptoms and examination
  • Clear explanation of findings and limitations
  • Faster start to plaster, splint, medicines, or referral
  • Images can be stored for follow up comparison
Clinic first approach: We do not run this like a walk in imaging centre. The goal is meaningful imaging, not routine imaging.

When we usually advise an X ray

  • Falls or injuries where fracture is suspected
  • Persistent knee pain where arthritis staging helps planning
  • Joint swelling after injury that does not settle
  • Follow up review of fractures treated elsewhere
  • Selected back or neck pain cases where imaging changes the plan
Often no X ray: Simple sprains, muscle strain, and early pain patterns can be managed safely after examination without routine imaging.

What to expect on the same visit

  • Clinical examination first to decide if imaging is required
  • Simple explanation of which views are needed and why
  • Support during positioning to reduce pain
  • Images reviewed on screen with you
  • Clear next step plan, plaster, physiotherapy, or further tests if needed

Radiation safety and responsible use

  • Only required views are taken based on the clinical question
  • Extra caution in children and in women who may be pregnant
  • No routine repeat films unless it changes decisions
  • Images stored for follow up comparison when useful

For most adults, the benefit of a clinically needed X ray is far greater than the small risk.

Common questions about digital X ray

Do I always need an X ray for joint pain

No. Many soft tissue and muscular problems can be diagnosed with history and examination. X ray is advised when it changes treatment, such as suspected fracture or arthritis staging.

Is digital X ray safe

Digital systems use controlled amounts of radiation. Only required views are taken, and extra care is used in children and in women who may be pregnant.

Will you share the images if I need review elsewhere

Yes. If you need review at another hospital for surgery or a second opinion, images can be shared or printed so that you do not have to repeat the test.

Can I walk in only for X ray without consultation

The facility is mainly for patients who consult at OrthoCure. If you are coming only for an X ray, a brief clinical review is still advised so the right views are taken and the study is used meaningfully.
