Knee Arthritis Care

Evidence based care that favours exercise and lifestyle change before injections or surgery. At OrthoCure Bone and Joint Speciality Clinic, knee arthritis is managed by clear examination, practical advice, strengthening exercise, medicines when needed, and selected injections such as PRP and hyaluronic acid only when they offer meaningful benefit.

What we assess in knee arthritis

  • Pain on walking climbing stairs squatting or standing long periods.
  • Stiffness after rest or in the morning.
  • Swelling or sensation of instability.
  • Activity limits in daily life or work.
  • Footwear weight and lifestyle factors that worsen symptoms.

Our stepwise plan for knee arthritis

  • Step one Advice on activity footwear weight and daily modifications.
  • Step two Strengthening and flexibility work focused on quadriceps hip and ankle mechanics.
  • Step three Selected injections such as PRP or hyaluronic acid when symptoms persist despite proper exercise.
  • Step four Clear discussion of surgery only when pain disability and X ray changes are advanced.

Tools we may use

  • Digital X ray for severity grading.
  • Short course medicines for flare ups.
  • In house physiotherapy for strengthening and gait training.
  • PRP or hyaluronic acid for selected early to moderate arthritis cases.

Every plan is individual. No patient is pushed into an injection or surgery.

Lifestyle changes that protect the knee

  • Weight control which reduces load on the knee with every step.
  • Choosing steady paced walking over sudden intense bursts.
  • Using hand support while climbing stairs rather than pulling on the joint.
  • Avoiding deep squats and floor sitting if they flare pain.
  • Footwear that supports the arch and absorbs shock.

Many patients in Ambattur and Avadi feel better once daily habits are corrected even before injections are discussed.

Exercise for stronger and steadier knees

  • Quadriceps sets and straight leg raise in lying.
  • Slow sit to stand practice from a chair.
  • Hip strengthening to improve overall alignment.
  • Stretching of calf and hamstring to ease stiffness.

Exercises are taught in clinic and given as a clear printed or digital plan so patients know exactly what to do.

When injections are considered

  • Early to moderate arthritis where pain remains despite proper exercise and medicines.
  • Patients who wish to delay knee replacement but accept that surgery may still be needed later.
  • Clear X ray correlation between symptoms and joint changes.
  • After discussion of cost benefit and realistic expectations.

At OrthoCure PRP and hyaluronic acid are offered only in selected cases and always as part of a larger plan that includes exercise and lifestyle change.

PRP and hyaluronic acid explained simply

  • PRP uses your own blood processed to concentrate platelets.
  • Hyaluronic acid works as a lubricant like fluid in the joint.
  • Both aim to reduce pain and improve function not to grow new cartilage.
  • Benefits can last months in some patients and less in others.

When knee replacement becomes the right step

  • Severe pain that limits sleep work and basic movement.
  • Stiffness and deformity that no longer respond to conservative care.
  • X ray showing advanced joint space loss and bone changes.
  • Failure of a full trial of exercise weight control medicines and injections where appropriate.

In such cases Dr Sumesh explains total knee replacement plainly and helps patients in Ambattur Avadi and nearby areas choose the right time rather than delaying until quality of life is lost.

Common questions about knee arthritis

Will exercise worsen the damage in my knee

Properly guided strengthening usually helps rather than harms knee arthritis. Movements are chosen to avoid sharp twisting or deep bending while still building muscle support.

Do all patients with knee arthritis eventually need replacement

No. Many patients manage well for years with exercise weight control medicines and sometimes injections. Replacement is for those whose pain and X ray changes remain severe despite full conservative care.

Is it safe to take pain tablets every day

Pain tablets have limits and side effects. They are used for short periods or on flare days and always after checking other medical conditions. Non medicine measures are always built around them.

Can PRP or gel injections regrow cartilage

Current evidence suggests that PRP and hyaluronic acid mainly calm inflammation and reduce pain in some patients. They are not proven to regrow lost cartilage.
