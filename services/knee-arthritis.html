Knee Arthritis Care

At OrthoCure Bone and Joint Speciality Clinic, knee arthritis is managed with a stepwise plan. Exercise and lifestyle change come first. Medicines and physiotherapy come next. Selected injections such as PRP and hyaluronic acid are offered only when they are likely to add real benefit. Knee replacement is discussed calmly when conservative care has been done properly.

What we look for in knee arthritis

  • Pain on walking, climbing stairs, squatting, or standing for long periods
  • Stiffness after rest or after sitting for some time
  • Swelling, grinding sensation, or a feeling that the knee may give way
  • Limits in daily activities, work, home duties, and social life
  • Body weight, footwear, and habits that overload the joint

Examination and X ray when required help grade severity and match treatment to your stage.

Early knee arthritis may show cartilage thinning, joint space narrowing, and small bone spurs

Stepwise plan for knee arthritis

  • Step one Advice on weight, daily activity, and safe movement patterns
  • Step two Strengthening and stretching focused on quadriceps, hip control, and ankle mechanics
  • Step three Short courses of medicines for flare days when needed, never as the only treatment
  • Step four Selected injections when symptoms remain strong despite proper exercise

The aim is simple. Less pain, more confidence in walking, and a better quality of life without rushing into surgery.

Exercise for stronger and steadier knees

Simple home exercises support the joint when done with correct technique

  • Quadriceps sets and straight leg raise in lying
  • Slow controlled sit to stand from a chair
  • Hip and core strengthening to improve alignment
  • Stretching of calf and hamstring to reduce stiffness

When injections are considered

  • Early to moderate arthritis where pain remains despite exercise and physiotherapy
  • Patients who want to delay knee replacement but understand surgery may still be needed later
  • Symptoms and X ray changes match clearly
  • After discussion of cost, expected benefit, and realistic outcomes

At OrthoCure, PRP and hyaluronic acid are offered only in selected cases and always as part of a broader plan.

PRP and hyaluronic acid in simple terms

Injections mainly reduce inflammation and improve comfort. They do not rebuild lost cartilage

  • PRP uses your own blood processed to concentrate platelets
  • Hyaluronic acid acts like a lubricant similar to joint fluid
  • Both aim to reduce pain and improve function in some patients
  • Benefits vary. Results are not guaranteed and exercise remains essential

When knee replacement is the right next step

  • Severe pain that limits basic movement, sleep, or work
  • Marked stiffness and deformity that no longer respond to conservative care
  • X rays showing advanced joint space loss and bone changes
  • Failure of a full trial of exercise, weight control, medicines, and injections when appropriate

Common questions

Will exercise worsen the damage in my knee

Correctly guided strengthening usually helps rather than harms. Movements are chosen to avoid sharp twisting and deep bending while still building muscle support around the joint.

Do all patients with knee arthritis eventually need replacement

No. Many patients manage well for years with exercise, weight control, medicines, and sometimes injections. Replacement is considered when pain and X ray changes remain severe despite full conservative care.

Is it safe to take pain tablets every day

Pain tablets have limits and side effects. They are used for short periods or for flare days after checking other medical conditions. Non medicine measures reduce long term reliance.

Can PRP or gel injections regrow cartilage

Current evidence suggests they mainly reduce inflammation and pain in some patients. They are not proven to regrow lost cartilage and they are never presented as a cure.
