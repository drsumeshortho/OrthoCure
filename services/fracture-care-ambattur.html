Fractures we commonly treat

  • Wrist (distal radius/Colles’), forearm
  • Clavicle (collarbone)
  • Ankle/foot, toe phalanx
  • Metacarpal/phalangeal (hand/fingers)
  • Stress fractures (early)

What to do before you arrive

  • Elevate the limb and apply a cold pack (10–15 min on/off)
  • Don’t try to “set” the bone yourself
  • Remove rings/watches from swollen hands
  • Bring previous X-rays/reports if available

Emergency note: open injuries, numbness, or severe deformity → seek urgent hospital care.

FAQs — Fracture Care in Ambattur

Do you have X-ray on-site?

Yes. Our on-site digital X-ray allows quick, accurate diagnosis and treatment decisions.

Will I need surgery?

Not always. Many fractures heal well with proper reduction and immobilisation. We explain options and escalate only when needed.

How long does a cast stay?

Typically 3–6 weeks depending on the bone and fracture pattern. We advise when to start movement and physiotherapy.

Is Sunday open?

Yes, Sunday 11 AM–1 PM for reviews and follow-ups.
