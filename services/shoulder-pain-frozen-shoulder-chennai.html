Shoulder Pain and Frozen Shoulder Care

Shoulder pain can disrupt sleep, limit dressing, and make daily life frustrating. At OrthoCure, the approach is conservative first. Frozen shoulder is handled with a stage based plan that reduces night pain, restores movement safely, and avoids unnecessary procedures. Tendon pain patterns are assessed clearly and managed with physiotherapy and a realistic home routine.

When to suspect frozen shoulder

  • Night pain that wakes you up or prevents side sleeping
  • Progressive stiffness in more than one direction
  • Difficulty combing hair, reaching overhead, or reaching behind the back
  • Slow onset without a major injury
  • Diabetes or thyroid disease in the background

The goal is calm clarity. Most cases improve without surgery with the right plan and patience.

What we check in the clinic

  • Range of motion pattern, especially external rotation and overhead reach
  • Rotator cuff strength and pain points
  • Neck screening when pain patterns suggest referred pain
  • Daily function and sleep disruption severity
  • Diabetes and thyroid context because recovery can be slower without control

What patients commonly confuse it with

  • Rotator cuff tendon pain
  • Impingement like pain with overhead activity
  • Acromioclavicular joint pain at the top of the shoulder
  • Neck related pain radiating to the shoulder

Correct diagnosis protects you from random exercises that make things worse.

Stage based plan for frozen shoulder

  • Early painful phase, focus on pain reduction and gentle motion, avoid forcing
  • Stiff phase, consistent guided stretching and a daily home routine
  • Recovery phase, progressive strengthening and return to function

When injections are considered

  • When pain is blocking sleep and exercise progress
  • When a short targeted intervention improves rehab participation
  • Always combined with physiotherapy and home routine
  • Never presented as a cure by itself

Common questions

Is frozen shoulder dangerous

It is not usually dangerous, but it can be very painful and disruptive. Most cases improve with time and the right plan.

Why is shoulder pain worse at night

In frozen shoulder, the capsule inflammation becomes more noticeable when the arm stays still for long periods. Lying on the shoulder also increases pressure and triggers deep aching pain.

Does frozen shoulder always need surgery

No. Most people improve without surgery using a stage based approach with pain control, physiotherapy, and a consistent home routine.

Do I need an MRI

Not always. Many shoulder problems are diagnosed clinically. Imaging is chosen when the pattern suggests another condition or if progress is not as expected.
