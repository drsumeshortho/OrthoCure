Physiotherapy at OrthoCure Thirumullaivoyal

Physiotherapy at OrthoCure Bone and Joint Speciality Clinic is built around one idea. The right exercise at the right time can do more for joints than any tablet. Plans are supervised, individual and closely linked to your orthopaedic diagnosis so that every visit moves you one step closer to comfortable movement.

Why physiotherapy is central to your plan

  • Supports the conservative first approach used for knee arthritis, shoulder problems and spine pain.
  • Strengthens muscles that protect joints and reduce load on painful areas.
  • Improves balance, confidence and endurance in daily life.
  • Helps after fractures and surgery so that stiffness does not become permanent.
  • Reduces long term dependence on pain medicine where possible.

Every exercise is chosen to match your age, X ray findings and general health. No copy paste routines.

Conditions we commonly support

  • Knee arthritis and knee pain in walkers, homemakers and office workers.
  • Shoulder pain from rotator cuff problems, frozen shoulder and overuse.
  • Spine and neck pain related to posture and disc strain.
  • Post fracture and post plaster stiffness of wrist, elbow, knee and ankle.
  • After knee or hip replacement when guided rehab is essential.

How we assess and plan

  • Joint and muscle examination with review of X rays and reports.
  • Discussion of your work, home duties and what you want to return to.
  • Setting a clear goal such as climbing stairs, sitting on the floor or walking a set distance.
  • Designing a stepwise exercise plan with home and in clinic components.
  • Review at regular intervals to progress or modify based on response.

Doctor and physiotherapist work together so that your plan stays medically safe and realistic.

What a typical session looks like

  • Brief check of pain level, stiffness and any new symptoms.
  • Warm up and gentle mobility for the involved joint.
  • Targeted strengthening and balance work using simple equipment.
  • Stretching and cool down with advice on pain control measures at home.
  • Written or video based home exercise instructions when needed.

Most sessions last around thirty minutes with focused work rather than long passive treatments.

Facilities available in our physiotherapy room

  • Exercise mats, step platforms and stationary supports for safe balance work.
  • Elastic bands and small weights for graded strengthening.
  • Parallel support and chairs for sit to stand and gait training.
  • Simple tools for hand, wrist and shoulder mobility.

Equipment is kept minimal but effective so that most exercises can be copied at home with simple objects.

Pain relief and adjunct modalities

Modalities can ease pain and stiffness so that you can exercise better. They are always secondary to active work.

  • Interferential therapy and TENS for selected pain patterns.
  • Ultrasound therapy for certain tendon and soft tissue conditions.
  • Wax bath for hand and wrist stiffness where indicated.
  • Cold or warm packs with guidance on safe use at home.

When physiotherapy must be modified or delayed

  • Uncontrolled fever, infection or recent major illness.
  • Sudden increase in swelling, redness or severe night pain.
  • Suspected fresh fracture or ligament injury that has not yet been assessed.
  • Very high blood pressure, cardiac issues or breathlessness not yet evaluated.
  • New nerve symptoms such as loss of power or marked numbness.

In such cases the doctor review is done first and physiotherapy is started only when it is safe.

Common questions about physiotherapy

Do I need a doctor consultation before starting physiotherapy

For most joint problems a doctor consultation is recommended so that serious causes such as fracture or infection are not missed. At OrthoCure plans are created with input from both doctor and physiotherapist.

How many sessions will I need

This depends on the condition and your goals. Some problems settle in a few guided visits while others such as long standing arthritis or post operative rehab need a longer plan. You will be told a realistic range at the start.

Will I have pain after exercises

Mild muscle ache is common when you start new work. Sharp joint pain or swelling is not expected and is monitored closely. Exercises are adjusted so that effort is felt in the muscles not as a deep joint pain.

Can physiotherapy replace surgery

In many early and moderate cases good physiotherapy delays or avoids surgery. In advanced disease it still improves strength and confidence and often helps patients recover better after an operation.
