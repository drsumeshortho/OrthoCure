PRP procedure at OrthoCure

PRP is prepared from the patient’s own blood under sterile conditions and injected only when clinically appropriate. Rehabilitation guidance is always included.

When PRP may help

  • Early to moderate knee arthritis
  • Selected shoulder tendon pain
  • Persistent symptoms despite physiotherapy
  • Patients aiming to delay surgery responsibly

For a full arthritis plan, see knee arthritis care.

What PRP does and does not do

  • May reduce inflammation and pain
  • May improve walking comfort and activity tolerance
  • Does not regrow lost cartilage
  • Does not replace surgery in advanced arthritis

Procedure and recovery

  • Blood sample collected and processed in clinic
  • Injection performed with sterile technique
  • Temporary soreness for one to two days is common
  • Exercise and load management matter most afterwards

Rehab support is coordinated through physiotherapy.

When PRP is not the right choice

  • Advanced arthritis with severe deformity
  • Complete tendon or ligament tears
  • Expectation of cartilage regrowth
  • Unwillingness to follow rehabilitation

In such cases, knee replacement may be discussed.

Common questions about PRP

Is PRP right for every knee or shoulder problem

No. PRP is usually considered for selected early to moderate knee arthritis and some shoulder tendon problems. Advanced arthritis, major tears, and significant deformity often need other treatments. Clinical assessment comes first.

Will PRP regrow cartilage

PRP is mainly aimed at reducing inflammation and improving symptoms in selected patients. It is not proven to regrow lost cartilage.

How many PRP injections are needed

Some patients may benefit from a single injection, while others may be advised a short series depending on the joint and response. The plan is decided after examination and discussion of realistic benefit.

Can PRP help me avoid knee replacement

In some patients, PRP can reduce pain and improve function and may delay the need for surgery. It does not replace knee replacement when arthritis is advanced and daily life is significantly limited.
