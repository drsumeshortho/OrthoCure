Platelet rich plasma injections are offered at OrthoCure Bone and Joint Speciality Clinic only for selected knee and shoulder conditions. PRP is not a miracle cure. It is considered when exercise based care and medicines are not enough and when evidence suggests a reasonable chance of benefit.

PRP procedure at OrthoCure

PRP is prepared from the patient’s own blood under sterile conditions and injected around the joint when clinically appropriate. The injection is always combined with rehabilitation guidance.

When PRP may help

  • Early to moderate knee arthritis
  • Shoulder tendinopathy without full thickness tear
  • Persistent pain despite exercise and physiotherapy
  • Patients aiming to delay surgery responsibly

What PRP does and does not do

  • May reduce inflammation and pain
  • May improve walking comfort and activity tolerance
  • Does not regrow lost cartilage
  • Does not replace surgery in advanced arthritis

Procedure and recovery

  • Blood sample collected and processed in clinic
  • Injection performed with sterile technique
  • Temporary soreness for one to two days is common
  • Exercise and load management are essential afterwards

Risks and limitations

  • Variable response between patients
  • Small infection risk like any injection
  • May not provide meaningful benefit in some cases
  • Repeat injections only after careful review
